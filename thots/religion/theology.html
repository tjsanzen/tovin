my theology of everything

 

 

this is just what i believe about…

 

 

 

 God: The head of the three in one, the Holy Trinity, who created the universe and everything in it. God does not necessarily have a gender, and the name God is not actually a name, but simply a title, with God’s name being Yahweh, Jehovah, El Shaddai, and more. Yahweh is all powerful and all knowing, but governs themself through the laws created by Yahweh, as they must being a God of order and justice, as well as one of love and peace.

 

Jesus Christ: The son of Yahweh, both fully God and fully human, who was born to the virgin Mary. A more accurate translation of the name Jesus is “Yeshua.” The name Yeshua means “to deliver; to rescue.” Yeshua was fully human during his time on earth and felt the same temptations and issues as any person living during that time might have felt. He was likely average height, with dark skin, black hair, and dark eyes, as was common for men in that location and time. He performed many literal miracles, or acts of the supernatural, having both the power to heal physical wounds and forgive sins. After being crucified on the cross and buried, he was raised to life again after being in Hades for 3 days. After spending an additional 40 days on earth teaching, he returned to heaven, to be with Yahweh and the Holy Spirit until his second coming.

 

The Holy Spirit: The Holy Spirit is the third person of the trinity. Also, there from the start, and involved with the creation of the earth, the Holy Spirit is what we interact with, and whom acts as our communication line between us and Yahweh.

 

The Bible: The Bible is the inspired word of God, but because of human translation, has become manipulated and changed over the years to fit political agendas. However, the original version is still the inspired word of God. However, the Bible is more than that; the Old Testament is like a history book, telling us what happened, the Gospels are the story of Jesus and his commands to us on how to live with practical examples, the letters to the churches are highly contextual letters from which we can pull certain parts to apply to our own lives to help us with living as Jesus and the early disciples did, and the final book of the Bible is a prophecy of what is to come.

 

Worship: Worship is our way of showing love for God (being the trinity). Worship can be anything that is focused on loving God, from music, photography, writing, painting, hard labor, spending time with people, all the way to more interesting things like taking care of others, watching nature, or exercising. Essentially, anything that is done for God is a form of worship and anything that is done for any other reason is not worship. This would include doing things for the sake of tradition, attraction to others, or to make yourself feel good. It can however be as simple as just spending time with God and talking to God.

 

Prayer: Praying is sometimes a form of worship when you are talking to God in adoration but is not when you are talking to God more out of a place of supplication.

 

The Church: The church is not a building, a denomination, or a religion, but rather, the collected people group of every person who is a follower of Jesus Christ. Therefore, there is no right or wrong denomination as long as they are following Jesus and not worshipping anyone else, despite the fact that some of the ways they live out his commands may be slightly skewed.

 

Heaven: Heaven is the dwelling place of the trinity, and the place where followers of Jesus go when their mortal bodies die. It is likely that it is in a different realm or parallel universe, which possible may be traveled to via quantum physics. Upon dying, believers go immediately to heaven.

 

Hell: The eternal place of torment for Satan, his fallen angels, and anyone who does not go to heaven after judgement day.

 

Hades: The place where all the dead went pre-resurrection and the place where the unbelievers go post-resurrection until the day of judgement.

 

Human Equality: All humans are created equal, whether biologically male or female or anything in-between. This means that they should not be treated differently, discriminated against, or given special privileges solely based on biological sex, colour of skin, height, hair colour, or any other physical characteristic.

 

Marriage: Biblically, people are not considered married until they married, which gives an entire new meaning to the idea of adultery as we know it in the Bible, because the Biblical idea of adultery was a modern equivalent to having multiple spouses. Therefore, sex should be viewed as the same as getting married in terms of a commitment.

 

Homosexual Marriage: Similar to heterosexual attraction, it is only a sin if you are lusting or engaging in sexual behaviors outside of marriage. However, it is a sin to be sexually intimate or lust after a person of the same biological sex, which means it is perfectly fine to be in a abstaining homosexual relationship, granted that there is no lusting or sexual acts involved. However for two people of the same biological sex to love each other and be attracted to one another is not a sin.
