Something fascinating has been happing in the past few years. Some may call it the signs of the end times, others may call it mental illness, confusion, and others may call it an enlightening. Of course, I am referring to the entire topic surrounding gender identity, biological sex, and the gender spectrum. So what caused all of this (note that we are not referring to homosexuality, but simply gender identity/fluidity)? 

Firstly, let me define some terms I'll be using, and what they mean:

  • Gender Identity: A person's perception of having a particular gender, which may or may not correspond with their birth sex. 
  • Gender Spectrum: Male to Female, and anywhere in-between
  • Biological Sex: The biological part of our gender, determined before birth or by surgery 
  • Gender Fluidity: Relating to a person whose gender identity is not fixed 
  • Sexual Orientation: A person's sexual identity, in relation to the gender to which they are attracted. 
  • Gender Confusion: When your Biological Sex and Gender Identity do not align - independent of sexual orientation 


   Believe it or not, more of it may be due to a combination of simple labels and people not being very kind at times. I'll start out with a story; I have a pastel pink shirt which I absolutely love and wear all the time. I wore this particular shirt to my nephew's birthday party one day, and it didn't take for my my brother and sister-in-law (both Millennials) told me I looked gay wearing a pink shirt. Wait what? So my sexual orientation can be defined simply by what color of shirt I'm wearing? Obviously I know that my sexual orientation is not defined by what color shirt I decide to wear in the morning, but is it easier to argue with every single person about this, or easier to accept that I'm gay? Or what about for someone who perhaps has not done as much research as myself, and so believes that because he loves the color pink, he must really be a female inside; after all, that's what everyone else says. 

   This is, in my opinion, an example of how toxic Gender Roles and Gender Stereotypes have led to gender confusion. For years, society has told people "if you are a male, you must do this; if you are a female, you must do this." and this has had some hugely harmful impacts on our society. Take for example the situation with the color of my shirt; in the early 1900s, pastel colors in general were defined as infant colors, including but not limited to blue and pink. However, as time moved on, it was generally accepted that pink was a strong masculine color and blue was a more fragile feminine color. It wasn't until after the Second World war that it really started to be pushed that blue was masculine and pink was feminine, and didn't even really stick until the late 1980s as the result of a smart marketing ploy, playing on the human want to be accepted with their gender identity.

   So what if all gender confusion is simply due to a genius marketing strategy to convince people to buy clothing that was not interchangeable between the genders? Then, the advertisers and society began to press the idea so hard that to deviate from this generally accepted fact would make you an outsider, and suddenly you would have to change your gender identity to match the clothes you wanted to wear. Of course, this isn't specifically for the color of ones clothes; there are many areas in which we can see this. Why are "feminine products" such as lipstick and makeup so heavily marketed to women over men? Because they like it more? Or is it simply easier to continue to milk out the old social norms and continue to market off and build on them than try to redefine them, even if there is potential for more profit through redefinition?

   However, of course there are going to be people all over the gender spectrum, which perhaps would be better defined as a "personality spectrum;" that is, how effective the marketing has been on your personality; in my case, it hasn't been, as I still wear a lot of pink and enjoy more "feminine" activities such as painting my nails, spa days, and doing my hair. It is because of this that we have what I believe is gender confusion; people say that one can't be a male and wear lipstick, or can't be a female and enjoy farming, and because of this, the people who are discriminated against are led to believe that they were given the incorrect biological sex, and so change to a different position on the gender spectrum, no longer identifying as they once did.

   Who is really to blame for this? In some way or another, almost everyone is for giving into the system at one point or another, but particularly conservatives seem to be; should this (conservatives) part of society suddenly start accepting men who paint their finger nails and women who enjoy farming, would we suddenly see our "gender spectrum" shrink drastically, as people would no longer feel that they are being separated from doing what they love by their gender? It is a fascinating theory.
